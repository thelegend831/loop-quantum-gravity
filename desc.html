\(det(a) = det ||{a^a}_b||\)
\(=\epsilon_{abcd} {a^a}_0 {a^b}_1 {a^c}_2 {a^d}_3\)
\(=-\epsilon^{0123} \epsilon_{abcd} {a^a}_0 {a^b}_1 {a^c}_2 {a^d}_3\)
   Summing over all even permutations of \(pqrs\) (such that \(-\epsilon^{pqrs}=1\)) means every time the \(pqrs\) indexes are permuted an even number of times, so must the associated \(a\)'s be rearranged to match the original \({a^a}_0 {a^b}_1 {a^c}_2 {a^d}_3\). For this to happen the \(abcd\) indexes must be permuted an even number of times to match the original expression. Because \(abcd\) incides have been permuted an even number of times, the \(\epsilon_{abcd}\) doesn't change its sign.
   Likewise when summing the odd permutations of \(pqrs\), \(-\epsilon^{pqrs}=-1\), the \(a\)'s need to be rearranged to match the original expression, the \(abcd\) indexes have to be permuted an odd number of times, and \(\epsilon_{abcd}\) changes its sign as well to \(-1\), so the term is each equal to the original expression.
   The total number of expressions summed is the number of permutations of 4 symbols, which is \({1\over{4!}}\).
\(=-{1\over{3!}}\epsilon^{pqrs} \epsilon_{abcd} {a^a}_p {a^b}_q {a^c}_r {a^d}_s\)
\(=-{1\over{4!}}\epsilon^{pqrse} \epsilon_{abcde} {a^a}_p {a^b}_q {a^c}_r {a^d}_s\)
